Marketa's
BED AND BREAKFAST

Located in the heart of downtown Victoria, Marketa’s Bed and Breakfast offers a unique taste of traditional Edwardian comfort and hospitality, mixed with European flair. Inside the heritage home built in 1912, guests can experience the romance of hand-crafted four poster beds and air jetted tubs to beautifully decorated family-style rooms.

Tripadvisor Reviews

Friendly and cute little place

Stayed just for 2 nights. As we were arriving late, a nice note with instructions and our key was left behind. Room was spotlessly clean with decent amenities. The B&B is located in a really nice neighbourhood just a short walk from Victoria harbour, so if you are short of time like us, it’s the perfect place to stay. Highlights/ the breakfast, served by friendly and efficient staff. Pancakes to die for and delicious eggs florentine. Such a warm stay and highly recommended to travellers from all over the world.

History

Marketa opened her B&B Victoria BC in 1996. With the help of her young son Jacob and Mr. T the lovable family dog (a real character!) she runs a warm and friendly hostelry accommodation with European flair, close to downtown Victoria. Her love of cooking is obvious to anyone who has sampled her gourmet breakfasts, which are a favourite of guests.
This beautiful Edwardian mansion was built in 1912. The first owner of the house was a widow with four children who bought it because she wanted something smaller. She was obviously wealthy because, even by today’s standards, the house is large.

Host

Marketa opened her bed and breakfast in 1996. With the help of her young son Jacob and staff, she runs a warm and friendly hostelry with European flair. Her love of cooking is obvious to anyone who has sampled her gourmet breakfasts, which are a favourite of guests.
Marketa was born in Prague and studied painting at University where she graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree. Her works are displayed throughout the house and you can often find her in her studio working on a new piece.
